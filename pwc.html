Operations & Supply Chain

2 systems in production
Building-materials manufacturer: demand forecasting
Listed manufacturer

Ordering ran on spreadsheets and instinct against long supplier lead times. Every forecast miss became dead stock or a stockout weeks later, and nobody could say which SKUs to trust.

Agents deployed
  • Ingestion agent: normalises 36 months of order history across SKUs, channels, regions; schema drift handled at the connector.
  • Seasonality agent: decomposes each series into trend, seasonal index, and promotion uplift before any model sees it.
  • Forecast agent: gradient-boosted ensemble over the decomposed series; 12-month rolling horizon with lead-time-aware reorder signals per SKU.
  • Backtest agent: rolling-origin cross-validation, 12 folds, every cycle; publishes WAPE and bias against held-out actuals.

US legal marketplace: lead intelligence & cadence
USA · $80M business

A large sales floor spreading effort evenly across leads regardless of quality. High-intent prospects and dead ends got the same follow-up, and nobody could defend why.

Agents and models deployed

  • Call assist: surfaces context and next-best action in the workflow the reps already use, Salesforce-native.

Platforms & Applied AI

1 system in production
UAE travel group: visa platform & travel assistant
UAE · longest partnership

Visa processing was manual and document-heavy, dying on errors nobody caught until submission. Support could not reason across a customer's own passport and visa history.

What we built
  • Document intelligence agent: OCR with confidence-routed extraction; low-confidence fields route to review, and nothing submits until it validates against country rules.
  • Visa workflow agent: a versioned rule engine per route and visa type; rule changes ship as versions, so past decisions stay reproducible.
  • Travel assistant: books, plans, and self-corrects with live context on visa and passport validity.
  • Platform: multi-tenant MongoDB with EntityId scoping on every query path; .NET Minimal API core; Next.js and React Native surfaces.

The backtest protocol, published in full
  • Holdout, frozen at kickoff. The most recent 12 months of history are sealed before any model work begins; nothing ever trains on them.
  • Rolling-origin evaluation. Each holdout month is forecast from an origin that sees only earlier data; the origin rolls forward and repeats. Twelve origins, twelve out-of-sample tests.
  • Accuracy and bias, gated together. Accuracy is reported as 100 minus WAPE; bias is gated at plus or minus 3 percent alongside it, so accuracy cannot be achieved through systematic over- or under-forecasting.
  • Baseline first. The incumbent method is scored under the identical protocol before ours; the claim is the difference, same data, same window, same math.
  • Re-proven every cycle. The backtest re-runs on every retrain and rule change. Any figure that fails re-validation is withdrawn.

PwC has the strategy, the client trust, the domain, and now agent OS to orchestrate and govern agents across vendors. What every engagement still needs is senior engineers who have shipped agent systems in production and can flex onto the work at PwC's assurance bar. Why PwC, specifically
  • agent OS orchestrates; someone still builds. agent OS coordinates and governs agents across vendors, with oversight built in. Shiftora builds them, MCP-native, so what we ship registers into agent OS and its oversight layer.
  • The bottleneck is senior build capacity. Advisory demand for agentic delivery outruns the supply of engineers who have run these systems in production. A co-delivery bench flexes that capacity up per engagement, without a permanent hire.
  • Alliances set the models; delivery still ships. The Anthropic and OpenAI alliances give PwC frontier models and a build platform. Turning them into a production system inside a client is the engineering, and that is the work Shiftora does.
  • Complementary, under your brand and gate. PwC owns the client, the domain, the methodology, and the assurance standard. Shiftora owns the engineering, working inside PwC delivery under a human gate, with IP assigned to PwC or the client.

The three things a platform build actually risks

The engineering risk in productising IP is not the idea; it is tenant isolation (one client's data never touches another's), versioning (a method that ships, diffs, and rolls back like software), and evaluation discipline (a module that must pass its golden set to deploy). These are the three things every system in 01 already demonstrates, and the reason they are safe to put in front of a client at a PwC assurance bar.

Precedent

Shiftora has already turned a specialist's judgement into a versioned, multi-tenant platform, a UAE travel group's visa system. The controller copilot above is the same anatomy applied to enterprise finance. The hard part of a multi-client delivery partner is tenant isolation, versioning, and evaluation discipline, the three things every system here demonstrates.
