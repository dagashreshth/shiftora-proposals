01

The logistics loop we run in production

This is not a pilot and it is not a demo; it is Scorpion’s industry and workflow, running live at an enterprise logistics operator today. The discipline is simple to state: a measured number, a human gate, and weekly accuracy reporting. This section covers how it runs and what the same anatomy would look like wrapped around a parcel network.

Exception handling on a continuous review loop
enterprise logistics operator · in production

Document and exception handling leaned on operator judgement, applied one case at a time, at national volume. We put that judgement on a supervised weekly loop: the system decides with a confidence score, anything uncertain routes to an operator, and every operator correction becomes training data, so accuracy improves in production rather than being claimed at launch.

How the loop runs
  • Confidence-scored decisions: every item the system touches carries a score; high confidence executes with a full audit trail, low confidence queues for a person.
  • Corrections become training: what operators fix each week is exactly what the next week’s model learns; the loop cannot drift silently.
  • Accuracy as a monitored curve: reported weekly against production traffic, not a benchmark set; a figure that stops passing its test stops being claimed.
  • A human gate throughout: nothing consequential executes automatically; the system earns autonomy queue by queue as its numbers hold.

What the loop replaces

  • Judgement held in operators’ heads becomes configuration: NDR actions, escalation thresholds, and client comms rules are defined by ops staff, not engineers, and versioned like code.
  • Chasing by phone becomes watching by system: gaps, stalls, and promise-risk are detected and escalated; people call only where a call adds judgement.
  • Errors found at billing become validation at entry: a POD or COD entry that breaches its rules cannot advance into an invoice or a claim.
  • Status enquiries become proactive notification: the shipper hears about the exception, and the fix, before they ask.

Why this anatomy fits Scorpion

  • The judgement already exists. Twenty-plus years of ops knowledge across 73 branches and 105 franchisees becomes configuration every office executes identically.
  • It rides on what is built. Scorpion already runs a TMS, portals, and e-POD; the loop consumes their data and writes back only behind approvals. Nothing is replaced.
  • Volume is the asset. 2.4 million packages a month means the loop learns faster here than almost anywhere; every week of corrections compounds.
  • Scale stops costing linearly. Growth loads the system, not the coordination headcount.

03

What we see at Scorpion

Four observations drawn from Scorpion’s published footprint and from how express networks at this scale typically run, offered as the lens we would bring rather than as settled conclusions; the people running the operation will see further than we can from outside.

Observation 01 · control tower

The network is watched by calling it.

Scorpion’s own network page describes transit monitoring through GPS, mobile tracking, and calling. Across 550 vehicles and 190 routes, the calling layer is a cost line, a response-time floor, and the first place volume growth hurts.

Observation 02 · exceptions

Exceptions move at email speed.

Support commits to a 24-48 hour email response, and NDR, claims, and escalations are judgement applied one case at a time. Industry benchmarks put every failed attempt at Rs 300-400 before RTO risk at double freight.

Observation 03 · cash & paper

Money moves on three modes across 178 points.

COD, FOD, and DOD at 65,000 LRs a month, reconciled across 73 branches and 105 franchisees, is a month-end assembly exercise today; the data to generate it continuously already exists in the TMS and e-POD.

Observation 04 · the network

Consistency depends on people, office by office.

105 franchisees and 140 delivery associates run on training and supervision. Rules as configuration would make every point in the network execute the senior team’s playbook identically, and update it overnight.

Context

In express cargo, margin lives in the exceptions. At 2.4 million packages a month, single-digit improvements in failed attempts, claims leakage, and reconciliation effort compound into crores a year, and every manual minute removed accrues directly to a thin-margin P&L.

  • Weeks 3-4 · human-gated live: one hub or lane, with Scorpion’s team approving every action; accuracy is reported weekly from then on.
